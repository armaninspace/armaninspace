Simple Example on a Decision Rules, Admissibility, etc.

We start with θ>0θ>0 and E[X|θ]=θE[X|θ]=θ and Var[X|θ]=θ2Var[X|θ]=θ2

We have a class of decision rules that are S={δc(X)=cX,0<c<1}S={δc(X)=cX,0<c<1}

and we’re give the standard squared loss funciton L(θ,a)=(θ−a)2L(θ,a)=(θ−a)2

To find a decision rule in SS that is admissible means finding a decision rule δcδc for there exists no other decision rule that is R-better than δcδc

We can find such a decision rule by finding δcδc for which the Risk is the minimum possible.

We know that the Bayesian risk for a decsion rule is defined by R(θ,a)=E[L(θ,a)]R(θ,a)=E[L(θ,a)]

Thus if L(θ,a)=(θ−a)2L(θ,a)=(θ−a)2, we can substitute that back into the risk formula and get: R(θ,a)=E[(θ−a)2]R(θ,a)=E[(θ−a)2]

Further we know that aa is of the form δc(X)=cXδc(X)=cX for c∈(0,1)c∈(0,1) and if we also plug that in we get R(θ,δc)=E[(θ−cX)2]R(θ,δc)=E[(θ−cX)2]
